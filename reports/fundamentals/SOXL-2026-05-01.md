## Product overview
- **SOXL** = Direxion Daily Semiconductor Bull 3X Shares.
- **Underlying:** ICE Semiconductor Index (proxy for SOX / PHLX Semiconductor). Tracks ~30 names.
- **Leverage:** 3x DAILY reset (geometric path-dependent — NOT 3x of long-term return).
- **Expense ratio:** ~0.75% annual.
- **Borrow / swap cost:** embedded financing drag ~SOFR + spread; in a ~5% rate environment this is roughly an additional 8-10%/yr drag on top of the ER (3x exposure means 3x the financing cost).
- **Structure:** uses total return swaps + futures; daily rebalanced.

## Structural risks of holding SOXL >1 day (CRITICAL)
1. **Volatility decay (beta slippage):** in choppy markets, 3x daily reset compounds losses. Example: SOX -5% then +5% on consecutive days = SOX flat (-0.25%), but SOXL = (1-0.15)(1+0.15) = -2.25%. Over a year of 30% realized vol, decay can eat 15-25% even with flat underlying.
2. **Path dependency:** SOXL 5y total return < 3x SOX 5y total return in almost every realized window with vol > 25%. Past drawdowns: -85% (2022), -65% (Aug-Oct 2024 example), -40%+ pullbacks routine.
3. **Financing drag** (~8-10%/yr) compounds.
4. **Reverse splits** historically used (1:15 in 2025-style scenarios) — does not change economics but signals path of pain.
5. **Not suitable for 5-year hold** per prospectus — Direxion explicitly markets as "trading tool, not buy-and-hold."
