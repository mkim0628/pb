## E. Bear vs. Bull Cases

### Bear case: Multiple compression dominates

- Fwd P/E **~109x** (FY26 EPS ~$1.32) vs SaaS large-cap median **~18x** and hyperscaler median **~28x**. Stock cannot sustain 50%+ premium without sustained 50%+ revenue growth вҖ” and Q1'26's +74% is *already* the easy comp; H2 comps tighten.
- US-Comm +137% Q4 is a **once-in-a-cycle** print; sequential deceleration is mathematical. Even if Q1 prints +110%, the deceleration trajectory becomes the headline.
- SBC **~20%+ of revenue** вҖ” share count creep ~3вҖ“5%/yr, no material buyback offset, large insider 10b5-1 sales (Karp, Cohen, others).
- Software-sector beta cohort (NOW, CRM, IBM) all derated in late April; PLTR is highest-multiple software, gets hit hardest in broad de-rate.
- Bear PT range: **$95вҖ“$110** (12x EV/sales on $7.5B FY26).

### Bull case: AI inflection + government windfall

- AIP commercial conversion is **anomalously fast** for enterprise software (typical 18вҖ“24 months вҶ’ PLTR 3вҖ“6 months). If Q1 shows TCV >$4.5B, this is no longer a pilot story.
- US Gov segment leveraged to DoD AI spending, **2026 budget cycle** explicitly funds Maven-era AI programs; recent USDA $300M contract validates civil-side expansion.
- Rule-of-X = ~115 (growth + FCF margin) вҖ” top of all software, period. Justifies 2x peer multiple even on 30%+ growth.
- Wedbush PT $230 (+60%) implies stock follows revenue trajectory rather than re-rates.
- Bull PT range: **$190вҖ“$220** (20x EV/sales, FY27 ~$11B path).

### Resolution

Bear case is *true at some price*; the question is whether $144 is below/at/above that. With FY26 already guided $7.2B and likely tracking $7.4B, EV/Sales at $144 is ~17x вҶ’ already inside the "base" zone. **Stock has done much of the de-rate work YTD**; further compression requires a fundamental crack, not just multiple normalization.
